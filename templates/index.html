5 am - tons of people coming into the West Loop area near Canal St (more than you might expect). Then again, this is where the Ogilvie Metra station is, as well as Union Station. Interestingly, most trips arriving here are from the Metra's Millennium Station. These travellers could happen to all work in this area but our guess is they're Divvying over to embark on the next leg of their commute.

























6 am - West Loop still a popular area. But people are definitely coming from more varied places. Looks like people coming into the Loop from the north on the Blue Line may be getting off early at Grand and using Divvy for the rest of their trip. For people needing to get to the west part of the Loop, this makes total sense since the next stop after Grand is way to the east at Clark/Lake, and you'd need to wait a while for the train to loop back to the west.

























7 am - two more hotspots have appeared! Now it's the people who take the Metra/Amtrak into Union Station and Ogilvie that make up the majority of those Divvying into the Loop. That's probably commuter volume in general, but it's interesting that so many people use Divvy for that last leg to get to work since it's a (relatively) short distance. And then there are those continuing their journey onto the Metra Electric at Millennium Station.

























8 am - the hotspots in the Loop have split into smaller groups. The two rightmost Loop spots are still most frequently visited by people coming from Union Station/Ogilvie, but the bottom left Loop spot is new and not as popular a destination for Union Station/Ogilvie Divvyers. Close enough to walk, probably. But, it's an unexpectedly popular destination for Divvyers coming directly east from Greektown, from the south near the Harrison Red Line stop, AND from the north near Chicago Ave. Popular place to work or popular... tourist attraction? Willis Tower is right there! Probably both, but if touristy, our guess is that you'd be able to see seasonal fluctuations in this spot's heat. The Millenium Station hotspot is now also slightly more north - perhaps indicative of tourists travelling to Michigan Ave rather than commuters trying to get to the Metra?

























9 am - no new hotspots. The Harrison and Jackson Red Line stops may be a large part of why lots of people are starting Divvy trips at the southeast part of the Loop. Most popular destination hotspots for these folk are the Willis Tower hotspot, Millenium Station, and Ogilvie/Union Station. Interestingly, not really staying inside the Loop in a commuting-to-work fashion if we assume Willis Tower is a tourist hotspot.













Divvy looks like it's been very useful for people trying to smooth out their work commutes, especially in making public transit connections that are otherwise awkward or inefficient. For instance, the transfer from Millennium Station to Union Station, or avoiding looping around the Loop to get around downtown Chicago.This is just a conclusion drawn from looking at weekday AM rush period Divvy trips.
